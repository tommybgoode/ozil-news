Who will play Özil in rumoured 2022 biopic?

By Jamie Waddell

May 22, 2020

ÖzilNews’ Hollywood sources attach veteran director David Fincher to Özil biopic. Keep this one off the record, on the QT, and very hush-hush, Özil fans but, according to persons familiar with the matter, the oft-rumoured Mesut Özil biopic is finally set to enter pre-production in summer 2021.

As this reporter understands it, two-time Oscar winning director David Fincher is attached to direct, with Ethan and Joel Coen (four Oscars between them) adapting Aaron Sorkin’s aborted 2017 screenplay. The promise of a Fincher-Coen collaboration is sure to whet the appetites of Özil fans looking for a gritty, surrealistic take on the star footballer’s early life and career, but the real news is yet to be revealed: who will play the great man himself? Here’s my pick of who’ll be on the casting call to don an Özil shirt in 2022.

Rami Malik

Following his Oscar-winning turn as Freddie Mercury in 2019’s Bohemian Rhapsody, Rami Malek certainly has the acting chops to portray Özil, but those chops are also where the problems lie. Özil is known for his beautiful, otherworldly eyes, and no disrespect to Mr Malek’s admittedly strange peepers, but that square jaw and chin of his massively distracts. I’m not discounting him from the race, and it’s exactly the kind of gutless, money-grabbing move Hollywood is known for, but with Fincher and the Coen brothers at the helm, I’d hope they’d look past the obvious.


Young Steve Buscemi

“I come to ÖzilNews.de for real Özil news, not time travelling nonsense”, you may be thinking, but hear me out. Last year’s Martin Scorsese megahit The Irishman proved that enhancements in digital technology have made transforming older actors into their younger selves a convincing and viable cinematic approach. In years gone by, when the idea of an Özil film seemed quite far-fetched, chatter around the ÖzilNews.de office placed Steve Buscemi as our top fantasy pick to play Özil. The reasons (and there are two bulbous ones), are obvious. Aesthetics aside, Buscemi is a hugely well-regarded actor in his own right. With his prior experience working with the Coens, alongside some digital trickery, could put him in good stead for a career-defining role in the next two years.


This Özil lookalike kid

What more can I say? He’s got the look, he loves gaming just as much as the real Ozil (See Video Section), and they probably won’t have to pay him much. If he’s not included in flash back or early life scenes of Özil I’ll consider the film a failure.

Mezut Özil

Mesut Özil is talented at a great many things, but is he an actor? We at ÖzilNews.de believe Özil has the potential to do anything. If the Hollywood bigwigs have the courage to take a chance on an untested actor like Mesut Özil, we’re supremely confident this man can deliver on the stage as he does on the pitch day in day out. Give the people what they want! It’s the role he was born to play!
