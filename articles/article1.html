Who Made Özil Sad?

By Tom Blackbourn

Apr 27, 2020



There is nothing that upsets us here at OzilNews more than a photo of an upset Özil. And this photo of a forlorn looking Özil on the bench for Arsenal this week did just that. As I strolled into the office on Monday morning I knew immediately something was wrong; the signs were all too obvious. Very Junior Reporter Chris staring vacantly into his morning coffee, unable to even respond to my traditional Monday morning greeting of 'Good Mezut, team!'. Chief Özil correspondent Jamie attempting to wipe away a solitary tear before meeting my gaze. Creative Director Alix biting back the sobs. The whole team were clearly in a state of shared tristesse that only comes when news of a sad Özil hits. With rumours abound as to the source of Özil's sadness I set out to explore the two most prominent theories and get to the bottom of why Özil is so sad. And perhaps in doing so lift the team out of their sad Özil funk.

The Cheese Sandwich Theory

One of the theories gaining serious ground amongst Özil fans is what has become known as 'The Cheese Sandwich Theory'. As our long-time readers well know, and new readers will soon learn, there is nothing Özil enjoys more than a nice cheese sandwich. Nothing too fancy - a mature English cheddar on Kingsmill 50:50 perhaps. Maybe a touch (just a touch) of pickle as an oocasional treat. Rumours have been circling that manager Mikel Arteta has disapproved of Özil's cheese sandwich obsession, stating concerns that a penchant for pickle can soon lead to some stinky performances on the field. 'Stick to the approved diet plan!' Arteta could be heard yelling at half-time. Lining up for the start of the second half, the crumbs on Özil's shirt told the rest of the story.

20 minutes into the second half and with his performance unimproved Özil was quickly substituted. It is thought that on the way to the bench Arteta issued his final ultimatum - cut out the cheese or you'll never play again. This would certainly go some way to explain Özil's sadness. Could this spell goodbye to Gouda? Cheerio to Cheddar? Toodle-oo to Danish Blue?

“You can't win anything with Cheddar”
Mikel Arteta - Arsenal Head Coach
Özil's New Boots

The beady-eyed amongst us Özil fans will have noticed that Mezut was sporting brand new boots for the match. Shiny Adidas Predators manufactured on Özil's favourite side of the Aurach river. "Puma is rubbish. I'm not a Puma I am a lion" Özil could be heard muttering under his breath in the changing room before the game. Except in German. It seems not everyone in the dressing room was as impressed by the new boots as Özil. Dani Ceballos has been quoted by an inside source as saying "I think Özil's new boots look stupid" to a teammate in the tunnel. Opinions flared further when shortly into the second half Ceballos added "You're not Adi Dass-ling anyone with your performance either". The joke was poor, but it cut deep. Too sad to play on, Özil was promptly substituted.
